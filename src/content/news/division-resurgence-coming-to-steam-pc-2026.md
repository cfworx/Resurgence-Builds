Division Resurgence is officially coming to PC through Steam later in 2026. The official Division Resurgence account on X confirmed it on June 5, 2026, the Steam store page is already live, and the title is wishlist-ready right now.

Here is the part most people are missing: **the PC version is already playable today**. Since April 2026, you can download Division Resurgence for free through the Ubisoft Connect launcher with full crossplay and cross-progression with mobile. The Steam release is the next platform expansion, not the first PC release.

If you have been holding out for a real PC version instead of running the game through an Android emulator on your battlestation, you actually have two options now, with a third coming later this year.

## The Official Announcement

The Division Resurgence team posted the following on X on June 5, 2026 at 8:11 AM:

> "We're still busy improving #TDResurgence for PC players, but we're glad to say the game can already be wishlisted and coming to #Steam later this year! If you play on PC, wishlist and join us in New York."

The post is on the official [@TheDivMobile account](https://x.com/TheDivMobile/status/2062915196989882405) and includes a 21-second promotional video showing third-person shooter gameplay, the Steam logo with a large **"COMING TO STEAM FOR FREE"** banner, a snowy "Restricted Area" scene, and final branding for **Tom Clancy's The Division** with PC and handheld display imagery. As of writing, the post has 111.3K views, 743 likes, 101 reposts, and 51 replies.

The wishlist link in the post goes directly to the [Division Resurgence Steam page](https://store.steampowered.com/app/3749720).

## You Can Already Play on PC Through Ubisoft Connect

Before we get to the Steam details, this needs to be front and center. Per the official Ubisoft news post titled [Play Resurgence on PC: All You Need to Know](https://www.ubisoft.com/en-us/game/the-division/resurgence/news-updates/1rUYWPgMV9hXYZ6FcLOkpX/play-resurgence-on-pc-all-you-need-to-know) (published April 28, 2026):

> "The Division Resurgence is now available to play for free on PC via Ubisoft Connect."

The key facts from that Ubisoft page, quoted verbatim where possible:

- **Free to play** through the Ubisoft Connect launcher.
- **Download size:** around 17GB. Ubisoft recommends having at least 30GB free on your computer.
- **Full crossplay:** "The Division Resurgence is fully cross-play on PC and mobile (iOS and Android)."
- **Full cross-progression:** "using the same Ubisoft Connect account, you can continue your progression in the game from any device."
- **Play with friends across devices:** "You can also play with your friends on any device, as long as you are in the same regional server."
- **Input support:** "you can use your usual inputs to enjoy the game: either mouse and keyboard or a controller of your choice."

### How to Install Through Ubisoft Connect

Per the same Ubisoft page:

> "Simply log into the Ubisoft Connect launcher on your computer, search for The Division Resurgence in the Ubisoft games store and download it to your computer. The game will first download a launcher that will prompt you to continue downloading the game files."

That is the official path today. If you want to start playing on your PC this afternoon, this is how you do it. You do not have to wait for the Steam release.

### Why the Steam Release Still Matters

If the game is already playable on PC via Ubisoft Connect, why does the Steam launch matter at all?

- **Steam discoverability.** Tens of millions of players live in the Steam ecosystem. A Steam page massively expands the title's visibility.
- **Steam features.** Achievements, Steam friends list integration, Steam Input controller mapping, Steam overlay, Steam Deck verification potential, and your existing Steam library.
- **One-launcher convenience.** For players who already manage their entire library through Steam, no separate launcher to babysit.

Both options will likely coexist after the Steam launch, the same way many Ubisoft titles are available through both Steam and Ubisoft Connect.

## What the Steam Page Confirms

The Steam store page is live now and gives us a lot of factual information to work with. Here is exactly what it says, no guessing, no padding.

### Free to Play, Confirmed

The Steam description states: "Jump into this free-to-play looter-shooter RPG featuring tactical combat, deep progression, and an exclusive canon storyline, playable solo or with friends."

So yes, the PC version is free to play. Same business model as the mobile version. No surprise paywall.

### Release Date

Steam lists the release as **Coming soon**. The X post confirms **"later this year"**, meaning later in 2026. No specific date is published yet.

### Developer and Publisher

Both listed as **Ubisoft** on the Steam page.

### Genre and Features

- **Features listed:** Single-player, Multi-player

## What the Steam Page Does Not Confirm

Being honest about what we do **not** know is part of the job. The Steam store page itself does not currently state any of the following for the Steam version specifically:

- Specific release date (only "Coming soon" and "later this year" from the X post)
- Steam-specific controller support implementation (Steam Input, Steam Deck verification, etc.)

If and when the team posts more on any of these, we will update this article.

## Why This Is a Big Deal

A few things worth calling out, all factual, no editorializing on Ubisoft's strategy.

**Mouse and keyboard are already supported.** The Ubisoft Connect version already supports both mouse and keyboard and controller input, per Ubisoft's official PC info page. Steam adds Steam Input for more controller configuration flexibility.

**It expands the hardware ceiling.** Mobile silicon is impressive but finite. A PC build can scale to higher resolutions, higher frame rates, ultrawide aspect ratios (when supported), and longer play sessions without thermal throttling your phone into oblivion.

**It widens the player base.** With full crossplay already live between Ubisoft Connect PC and mobile, the Steam launch should funnel even more players into the same shared pool. Healthier matchmaking, more populated Dark Zones, more Conflicts queues, and faster Hostile Nest runs. Anyone who has waited five minutes for a Conflicts match at off-peak hours understands why this matters.

**It validates the title's longevity.** Going from mobile and Ubisoft Connect to also being on Steam is the kind of investment that signals the team is in this for the long haul. Pair that with the [Season 1, 2, 3 roadmap](/news/season-1-2-3-roadmap/) and the recently published [public Trello board](/news/division-resurgence-known-issues-trello-board-breakdown/) and you can see a team committed to communication and content cadence.

## Fan Response: What the Community Is Saying

Reading through the replies on the [announcement post](https://x.com/TheDivMobile/status/2062915196989882405), the community response is exactly what you would expect from an engaged player base on a live service title: a healthy mix of hype, questions, feature requests, and bug callouts. Here is what the replies look like.

### The Excited Camp

Plenty of straight-up enthusiasm. **@FalcoInAshes** summed up the PC crowd's reaction with: "oh my god steam port i genuinely love you all". **@Maverick__CW** followed up the announcement with a simple "DONE...!" (the universal language of "wishlisted"). And **@UbisoftMobile** itself dropped a "Let's go! 💥" in the replies. The Steam port lands well with players who want their library in one place.

### The Real Question Everyone Is Asking

The single most important question in the replies came from **@CharlesHarr80**: "Do we have to start over on steam if we are already playing on PC through Ubisoft Connect?" This is the question. Cross-progression between Ubisoft Connect PC and mobile is already confirmed (covered above), but whether the Steam release inherits the same Ubisoft Connect account integration has not been formally addressed yet. **@GottaWatchMe** and **@hmgamesnsoftwa1** raised the related point about whether the Steam version will still require the Ubisoft Connect launcher to run in the background, which is common for Steam-published Ubisoft titles.

If you are sitting on a Ubisoft Connect account with progression already in flight, you are not the only one waiting on the official word.

### Console Requests Are Loud

A noticeable chunk of replies asked about consoles. **@tellylord** asked about PS5 Pro: "Any chance of this gem coming to consoles ps5 pro preferably". **@killpax36** asked about Nintendo Switch 2: "Any plans y'all gonna come to Nintendo switch 2 since it's a portable console/hybrid system?" **@MichaelJ465** would prefer a console version over the current platforms. As of this writing, no console release has been announced. The current confirmed platforms are mobile (iOS and Android), PC via Ubisoft Connect, and PC via Steam later in 2026.

### Bug Reports and Quality of Life Requests

No surprise here, the replies also functioned as an impromptu bug tracker. **@JCyArG** flagged the "144/150 bug" and reported missing weapons from their stash ("My First Bloom and Iron Lung have vanished"). **@INMATEofARKHAM** asked whether broken side missions would be fixed by the Steam release. **@lcarocham** reported PC-specific issues with loot pickup and respawn flow.

Most of these align with active cards on the [public Trello known issues board](/news/division-resurgence-known-issues-trello-board-breakdown/), including the items-disappearing-from-inventory investigation and the Outpost Annihilation side mission fix that is currently In Progress. If you have not seen the Trello board yet, that is the right place to track whether your favorite bug is already logged.

The team also has gameplay polish requests landing in the same comment thread. **@ChitoGamingLive** asked for better graphics and optimization. **@Houseofwrynn81** asked for QoL changes like auto-skip cutscenes and a smoother chock-running input. **@Born2beSlicker** asked for continued attention to the mobile build alongside the PC push: "Can you improve it for mobile too? It's been quite a mess on mobile since the 1.2 update". Fair ask, and worth flagging given that the title was mobile-first and the mobile player base is still the foundation of the game.

### Regional Access Concerns

A few replies raised regional and language access. **@8o_pt** asked for Arabic language support to be added. **@yahya_heja9813** raised broader access concerns for players in restricted regions. The current Steam page lists English, French, Italian, German, and Spanish (Spain) at launch, with full audio only for English. Additional language support has not been announced.

### Division 2 Comparisons

**@Sr_Heretic** raised a thoughtful concern: "I hope this doesn't cause issues with Both Resurgence and The Division 2 being on the same platform and split players." Worth noting that these are different titles with different progression systems and player bases, so the overlap on Steam is more catalog adjacency than direct competition for shared progression. We will keep an eye on whether the team comments on this.

### Best One-Liner of the Thread

**@agent_irishbull** with a single line that every middle aged gamer dad immediately understood: "This game is affecting my marriage." Take that as either a warning or a recommendation depending on which side of the couch you are reading this from.

### Overall Tone Read

Looking across the replies as a sample: the response skews engaged rather than cleanly positive or negative. A meaningful chunk is excited about the Steam port. A larger chunk is using the announcement as a megaphone to surface bugs, request features, and ask the right questions about how Steam will interact with their existing Ubisoft Connect progression. That is healthy for a live service title at this stage. A quiet thread is the worst sign for any game launch. This thread is anything but quiet.

For the team, the takeaways from the replies are clear: confirm Steam <-> Ubisoft Connect cross-progression specifically for the Steam release, clarify whether the Ubisoft Connect launcher will run alongside Steam, and keep the mobile experience moving in parallel. The community is paying attention, and they are clearly invested.
