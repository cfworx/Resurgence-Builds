## The Thesis — Why Skill Crit Stacking?

Skill builds in Resurgence scale with three multiplicative stats:

1. **Skill Crit Chance** — the chance any skill tick crits
2. **Skill Crit Damage** — how much extra damage a crit deals (typically +100% to +200%)
3. **Skill Multi-Shot** — chance any skill tick fires an extra tick

These three stats stack **multiplicatively**, which means a build that pushes all three to 50%+ has a damage ceiling far above any single-stat build. The Jackpot gear set is the only set in the game that rolls **all three skill stats on its set bonuses**, which is why it is the foundation of every top skill build.

Add the **Fire Cycle T2 1pc** (+12% Skill Crit Damage), the **Brains Over Brawn OS** (+28% Engineering → scales every skill stat), and **Minion Tracking backpack** (+36% Skill Crit Damage on skill kill for 12s), and you have a skill that crits 50%+ of the time, hits with multi-shot on top of that, and does +250%+ damage on every crit.

RogueMadras's showcased stats:
- **Skill Crit Chance: 51%**
- **Skill Crit Damage: 158%**
- **Skill Multi-Shot: 8%** (with room to grow)

> Build philosophy: skill builds in this game are won on **multiplicative stacking**. Crit Chance × Crit Damage × Multi-Shot is the formula. Every gear slot must contribute to one of those three. Anything that does not is wasted.

## Chemical Trap — Why This Skill?

Chemical Trap is the **highest-throughput AOE skill** in the game when stacked with skill crit gear. The reasons:

1. **It deploys multiple traps** — not a single hit, multiple zones of acid
2. **Acid is a damage-over-time (DoT)** that ticks for several seconds — each tick can crit, each tick can multi-shot
3. **The acid Status Effect itself is a vulnerability layer** — enemies under acid take more damage from all sources
4. **It has a short cooldown relative to its damage output** — you can keep it up nearly 100% of fight time with skill cooldown stats

Compare to the alternatives:
- **Seeker Mine:** Single-target burst. Good but does not multi-tick.
- **Fixer Drone:** Healing, not damage.
- **Striker Drone:** Decent damage, but does not crit-stack as well.
- **Pulse:** Utility, not damage.
- **Riot Foam Chem Launcher:** CC, not damage.

Chemical Trap wins because **every tick of every acid pool can crit, can multi-shot, and can stack with the next tick.**

## The Math — One Chemical Trap Detonation

Assume RogueMadras's showcased stats:
- **Skill Crit Chance: 51%**
- **Skill Crit Damage: +158%** (i.e., crit damage multiplier 2.58×)
- **Skill Multi-Shot: 8%**
- **Engineering core stat:** ~900 (with Brains Over Brawn proc up to ~1,200)
- **Brains Over Brawn:** +28% Engineering (scales skill damage proportionally)
- **Minion Tracking:** +36% Skill Crit Damage for 12s post-kill (procs constantly in elite waves)

### Base Chemical Trap tick (normalized to 100):

**Step 1 — Engineering scaling:**
`100 × (1 + Engineering / 1000) = 100 × 1.9 = 190` (with ~900 Engineering)
With Brains Over Brawn proc: `100 × 2.2 = 220`

**Step 2 — Average crit multiplier (51% × 2.58×):**
`Average = (1 - 0.51) + (0.51 × 2.58) = 0.49 + 1.316 = 1.806`
Per tick (average): `220 × 1.806 = 397`

**Step 3 — Multi-Shot proc (8% chance of extra tick):**
`397 × 1.08 = 429`

**Step 4 — Minion Tracking active (post first kill, +36% SCD):**
Effective crit damage becomes 2.58 + 0.36 = 2.94×
Average crit multiplier: `0.49 + (0.51 × 2.94) = 1.99`
Per tick: `220 × 1.99 × 1.08 = 473`

### Now multiply by tick count:

Chemical Trap acid pools tick 6-10 times per detonation, and you typically deploy 2-3 trap zones per cast.

Per cast (average, Minion Tracking active):
- 3 zones × 8 ticks × 473 = **~11,350 damage per cast on a single enemy**
- AOE — every enemy in the pool eats the same DOT

Against a 5-enemy pack:
- **~56,750 damage per Chemical Trap cast on the pack**

With ~6 second cooldown (skill cooldown stat from gear and BoB proc), you cast every 6 seconds for sustained DPS of:
- **~9,500 DPS sustained against a 5-enemy pack**

That is the "highest DPS in EU servers" claim — and it scales linearly with pack size up to the cap of enemies the acid pool can affect.

## Skill Chip Combinations

The build runs Chemical Trap with **three supporting skill chips**:

### Chip 1 — Chemical Trap Intensity (or "the hand one")
This is the primary damage chip. **Intensity is the priority stat** on this chip. Radius and Duration are not important (RogueMadras explicitly notes this).

### Chip 2 — Acid Effect
The Acid Effect chip extends or strengthens the corrosion DOT. This is what makes Chemical Trap multi-tick.

### Chip 3 — Siphon
Siphon converts damage dealt by your skill into a survival layer (typically armor or skill cooldown). It is the survivability backbone of the build.

**Chip attribute priority:**
- **Priority:** Chemical Trap Intensity
- **Skip:** Radius, Duration (not important)

## Engineering Cores Explained

Every gear piece rolls a Core stat that strongly modifies one of three pools:
- **Firepower** — weapon damage
- **Toughness** — health / armor
- **Engineering** — skill damage

**Engineering cores on all 6 pieces is mandatory** for this build. Each Engineering core adds roughly 100-150 to your Engineering stat, and your final Engineering value directly multiplies your skill damage.

With 6 Engineering cores + Brains Over Brawn maxed, expect:
- **Engineering: 1,100-1,400**
- **Skill damage multiplier: 2.1× - 2.4× base**

**Do not mix cores.** A single Firepower or Toughness core anywhere on the loadout reduces your skill damage by 100+ points of Engineering — that is a 10-15% DPS loss.

## Combat Rotation

1. **Pre-engage:** Activate Brains Over Brawn (+28% Engineering, scales every skill stat).
2. **Open with Chemical Trap.** Place 2-3 zones at the entry chokepoint where the pack will move through.
3. **Acid DOT ticks** — every tick can crit, every tick can multi-shot.
4. **First kill triggers Minion Tracking** — +36% Skill Crit Damage for 12 seconds.
5. **Re-deploy Chemical Trap** when the cooldown is up (typically 4-6 seconds with Engineering and chip cooldowns).
6. **Nothing to Lose talent procs** as you take damage — chest reduces skill cooldown by up to 8.4% per stack (3 stacks max), keeping your Chemical Trap nearly always available.
7. **Pop Tech Operator signature** for the burst window — typically once per pack or once per elite.

The full cycle: **Acid pool → kill → +36% SCD window → deploy second acid pool → repeat.**

## Why This Build Farms T2 Elites

Elite missions in The Division: Resurgence are designed around **pack clearing** — multiple yellow/red elite spawns at once. Chemical Trap's AOE shines here:

- **Single-target shotgun builds** kill one elite then re-engage. Slow.
- **Marksman rifle builds** kill from range but one shot at a time. Medium.
- **Chemical Trap skill builds** drop one trap and damage **every enemy in the pool simultaneously** for 8-10 seconds. **Pack-clear speed is 3-4× faster than weapon-DPS builds against grouped enemies.**

The acid pools also bypass armor regeneration windows — the DOT keeps ticking even when the enemy is behind cover or trying to recover armor.

For T2 farming specifically (corrupted T2 mission drops), this is the fastest path. RogueMadras explicitly highlights this as the build's primary use case.
