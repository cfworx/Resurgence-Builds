# Jackpot Striker Drone Tech Operator Build | Division Resurgence Engineering Build

By RapidF5 · Patch 1.1.2.2 · Tech Operator · Updated June 1, 2026

## TL;DR

This is a **Tech Operator Offensive Operations** build that pumps every roll into Engineering and lets a **Striker Drone** do the killing. Four pieces of **Jackpot** for the Skill Crit Chance and Skill Crit Damage stacking, two pieces of **Fire Cycle** for another +12% Skill Crit Damage on top, and the **[Rampart OS Protocol](/database/os-protocols/rampart/)** sitting on +10% Damage per stack while you hold a corner. **Hunting M44** is the primary, **Mk16** as the swap. The Hunting M44 talents feed Skill Intensity back into the drone every magazine, and the Mk16 talents top off Engineering and Signature charge while you reposition. Press the drone, hold the line, watch the numbers turn red.

## What This Build Does

The whole build is wired around one question: how do you make a Striker Drone hit like a freight train? The answer is stacking Skill Critical Hit Chance and Skill Critical Hit Damage to the point that the drone is critting on most shots, and those crits are landing for absurd numbers. Then you turn the Tech Operator into a stationary turret yourself so Rampart pumps another +10% per stack of Damage on top of that.

A few things make this build tick:

- **Jackpot 4-piece** stacks +10.8% Skill Critical Chance and +24.0% Skill Critical Damage on top of all your Engineering-rolled gear attributes
- **Fire Cycle 2-piece** adds another +12.0% Skill Critical Damage, which double-dips on the Jackpot crit damage and turns Skill Crit into a real damage source instead of a stat-padding number
- **Rampart** rewards immobility, and a Tech Operator running a Striker Drone is naturally immobile while the drone does the work
- **Hunting M44** with Enhancement Trade 2 means every magazine you dump feeds +33.6% Skill Intensity back into the drone (+8.40% per 20% mag emptied, four stacks)
- **Mk16** with Torrential Downpour 2 gives you +31.50% Engineering for 12 seconds the moment you tag ten hits, and Rapid Charge gets your Signature back fast for Drone Overload procs

The role is Tech Operator Offensive Operations. You are the drone driver, not the gunfighter. Your rifle exists to feed the drone, not the other way around.

### OS Protocol

**Rampart** is the only choice that matters for this build. While immobile, grants +10% Damage for 1 second, up to 6 stacks. The bonus resets the moment you move. On a Tech Operator running a stationary drone, you are immobile by default while you direct the drone, so Rampart sits at or near six stacks during every fight. That is +60% Damage stacked on top of everything else.

If you do not have Rampart yet, **Surgical Engineering** is a serviceable fallback because it adds another Skill Critical Damage layer when your Skill Crit Hits proc. It will not match Rampart's ceiling, but it keeps the build functional while you grind.

### Skill Mods

The skill mods are all built around the Striker Drone with a Drone Overload synergy:

- **Stabilized Flight** · 2-piece +Skill Health 6.0%, 3-piece "Applying Drone Lock grants +9% Skill Critical Hit Chance for 20 seconds"
- **Optimized Targeting** · 2-piece +Skill Health 8.0%, 3-piece "Within 30 seconds of activating Drone Overload, Striker Drone hits have a 10% chance of granting +21.80% Skill Critical Hit Chance for 20 seconds"
- **Thermite Charge** · 2-piece +Skill Health 6.0%, 3-piece "Striker Drone Critical Hits have a 7.40% chance of exploding, dealing 300% damage"

The reason these three sit together is the chain reaction they trigger. Activate Drone Overload, hit something with Drone Lock, and you stack a +9% Skill Crit Chance buff (Stabilized Flight) plus a chance at +21.80% Skill Crit Chance (Optimized Targeting). Once the drone is critting, Thermite Charge starts rolling for 7.40% explosion procs at 300% damage on every crit. It is a feedback loop built into the gear.

## Why Each Gear Piece Matters

### Jackpot 4-piece

Skill Radius +12.0%, Skill Critical Chance +10.8%, Skill Critical Damage +24.0%. The 4-piece is what makes a Skill Crit build viable in the first place. The 3-piece adds the +10.8% Skill Crit Chance that, combined with your Engineering-rolled crit chance attributes, gets you over the threshold where the drone is critting on most shots instead of occasionally. The 4-piece +24.0% Skill Crit Damage is the multiplier those crits scale against.

### Fire Cycle 2-piece

+12.0% Skill Critical Damage. The cleanest 2-piece for a skill crit build, period. Twelve percent Skill Crit Damage stacked on top of the Jackpot 4-piece's 24%, the Glass Cannon ratios from your gloves and holster, and the chest and mask crit damage rolls is what pushes the drone numbers past the threshold where Hostile Nest bosses start melting.

### Chest: Jackpot with Injured Concealment 2

Max Health on the primary attribute is the only "defensive" line in the build, and Injured Concealment 2 turns getting shot into a damage buff. Hits taken grant +2.50% Damage for 10 seconds, up to 10 stacks. Ten stacks is +25% Damage just for getting tagged once a second for ten seconds, which is going to happen whether you want it to or not the moment you anchor for Rampart.

### Backpack: Jackpot with Overwatch 2

Skill Critical Chance + Skill Multi-Shot Chance on attributes, Overwatch 2 on talent. Overwatch 2 grants +21% Skill Damage when behind cover for at least 3 seconds. Behind cover. Holding position. Sound familiar? This stacks with Rampart's immobility clause and turns the Tech Operator into a vending machine for skill damage as long as you do not jog out of cover to chase loot.

### Gloves and Holster: Fire Cycle, Engineering, Skill Crit Chance + Skill Crit Damage

Both Fire Cycle pieces are rolled for the maximum amount of crit on each. There is no defensive line here, no utility. Pure offense rolls because the build is already getting its survivability from positional play and Injured Concealment stacks.

### Knee Pads: Jackpot, Engineering, Skill Crit Chance + Skill Multi-Shot Chance

The fourth Jackpot piece. Skill Critical Chance and Skill Multi-Shot Chance get rolled here because the kneepads slot does not have a talent line, so you milk the attributes for crit on both lines.

## Weapon Talents Explained

### Hunting M44 (T2 LMG) Talents

- **Enhancement Trade 2** · "Grants +8.40% Skill Intensity for each 20% of the Magazine Size emptied for 8 seconds, up to 4 stacks. Talent Cooldown: 1 second." Empty 80% of the magazine, get +33.60% Skill Intensity for 8 seconds. That is a flat damage and effect amplifier on the drone every time you dump a mag.
- **Ammo Supply** · "Shooting has a 54% chance of returning the bullet in the magazine if it contains less than 30% of its ammo." Once you are under 30% mag, you start getting bullets back at better than coin-flip odds, which lets you keep firing into the back of the magazine to extend the Enhancement Trade window.

The combo is intentional. Dump the front of the mag fast to ramp Enhancement Trade, then the back of the mag pays you back through Ammo Supply so the Skill Intensity buff stays live for longer.

### Mk16 (AR) Talents

- **Torrential Downpour 2** · "Hitting enemies 10 times within 5 seconds grants +31.50% Engineering for 12 seconds." The Mk16 fires fast enough that ten hits in five seconds is easy on any close-range target. Twelve seconds of +31.50% Engineering covers an entire engagement.
- **Rapid Charge** · "Hitting an enemy 5 times in 3 seconds grants 50 Signature Ability Charge." Free Signature charge for shooting things, which means Drone Overload comes back faster, which means Optimized Targeting's 30-second window keeps refreshing.

The Mk16 is your reposition weapon. Swap to it when you need to move, dump rounds into a target, get Torrential Downpour rolling, then go back to the M44 once you anchor for the next position.

## How to Play This Build

Tech Operator Offensive Operations rewards positional discipline. The Striker Drone needs line of sight, you need to be immobile for Rampart, and Overwatch 2 wants you behind cover. All three of those things point to the same playstyle. Pick a piece of cover with sightlines, drop the drone, and work the angle until the room is dead.

The opener sequence:

1. Anchor behind cover with sightlines on the engagement
2. Drop the Striker Drone with Drone Overload active
3. Hit the priority target with Drone Lock to proc Stabilized Flight's +9% Skill Crit Chance
4. Open up with the Hunting M44, dump 80% of the magazine into something to ramp Enhancement Trade 2 to four stacks
5. Stay immobile as Rampart builds to six stacks of +10% Damage (six seconds of holding still)
6. Optimized Targeting starts rolling Skill Crit Chance procs, Thermite Charge starts rolling explosion procs on drone crits, the room evaporates

When you need to reposition, swap to the Mk16, tag a target for Torrential Downpour 2 plus Rapid Charge as you move, then anchor again at the new position and reset the Rampart stack count. Rampart will reset to zero on every move, which is the only "tax" the build pays for being so immobile-friendly.

## Where to Run This Build

- **Hostile Nests** · The drone gets to chew on a packed room of targets, and the immobile playstyle suits a single anchored position behind cover
- **Weekly Bounties** · Anchor on the boss, let the drone work the boss while you feed it Skill Intensity from the M44
- **Conflicts (PvE side)** · Skill Crit builds scale with the higher Power Score brackets because Engineering rolls scale linearly while weapon damage hits soft caps
- **Dark Zone PvE rooms** · As long as you have a corner, Rampart is going to sit at six stacks

This is not a Dark Zone PvP build. Rampart's reset-on-move clause is a death sentence the moment another player breaks your line, and the build has zero burst damage outside the drone window.

## OS Protocol Backup Plan

If you do not have Rampart yet:

- **Surgical Engineering** (High-End) · Skill Critical Hits have a 60% chance of granting +16% Skill Critical Damage for 10 seconds, up to 3 stacks. This is the cleanest stand-in because it adds another Skill Crit Damage layer on a build that is already proccing Skill Crit constantly
- **Tactical Layering** (High-End) · Deploying a Skill grants +15% Engineering, up to 3 stacks. Easier to maintain than Rampart, lower ceiling
- **Ambusher** (Superior) · Being immobile for 3 seconds grants +22.50% Engineering. Same immobile-rewarding philosophy as Rampart at lower rarity

Rampart is the goal. Everything else is a placeholder until you roll it.

## Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best Tech Operator build in Division Resurgence?

A Jackpot 4-piece plus Fire Cycle 2-piece Skill Crit build using Hunting M44 and Mk16 is currently one of the cleanest Tech Operator Offensive Operations setups. The Striker Drone scales with Engineering, Skill Critical Chance, and Skill Critical Damage, all of which the Jackpot and Fire Cycle sets stack natively, and Rampart adds another damage layer on top.

Why is this build using Hunting M44 instead of an exotic?

Because the Hunting M44 T2 LMG carries Enhancement Trade 2 and Ammo Supply, which are the cleanest LMG talents for feeding Skill Intensity back into the Striker Drone every magazine. The exotic slot does not have a free-pass LMG yet that pulls double duty on Skill Intensity feeding the way this rifle does.

Can I run this build without Rampart?

Yes, but the ceiling drops. Surgical Engineering is the cleanest stand-in until you roll Rampart, followed by Tactical Layering. Both keep the build functional. Neither hits the +60% Damage ceiling that six stacks of Rampart provides.

Is the Mk16 better than the Vector for the secondary slot?

For this build, yes. The Mk16 with Torrential Downpour 2 gets to +31.50% Engineering after ten hits in five seconds, which is trivial at AR fire rates and stays up for twelve seconds. The Vector is faster to ramp talents but does not push the Engineering ceiling as hard. Mk16 is the right call when the goal is to feed the drone, not to gunfight.

How important is Skill Multi-Shot Chance on this build?

Skill Multi-Shot Chance is a free damage multiplier on the drone because each multi-shot proc fires another shot from the drone at no resource cost. The build runs Skill Multi-Shot Chance on the backpack and knee pads because they are slots where you do not need crit chance or crit damage on both attribute lines. Roughly 40% Skill Multi-Shot Chance is the sweet spot before diminishing returns kick in.

Is Division Resurgence pay-to-win?

Every gear set used here drops in the field. Jackpot and Fire Cycle pieces come from the standard High-End loot pool and the appropriate vendors. The premium track of the Classified Ops Pass is cosmetics, not power. Welcome to the genre.
