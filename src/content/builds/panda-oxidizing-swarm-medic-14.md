## What It Is

> 🎬 **Build credit:** This build comes straight from the mind of [**ProjectPandaTv**](https://www.twitch.tv/projectpandatv). Watch the breakdown here: [BEST ALL-AROUND BUILD in The Division Resurgence (PVE + PvP)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CB3Rq2VIP0g). Go drop him a follow!

Welcome to the **"Toxic Waste Dump"** Medic. If you thought Field Medics were just there to awkwardly throw healing hives while the Vanguard does all the actual fighting, you haven't seen ProjectPandaTv's Oxidizing Swarm build. This archetype is an Engineering-heavy "Oxy" build that treats the Geneva Convention like a coloring book and turns team fights into a localized hazmat emergency. This top-tier Field Medic Dark Zone PvP/PvE Oxidizing Swarm build is a high-difficulty setup designed to absolutely melt meta-chasers into green goo while you laugh from safety.

Whether you're running PvE, sweating in Conflict, or stealing loot in the Dark Zone, this build is designed to nuke players almost instantly. You'll be dropping Oxidizing Swarm hives that last nearly a full minute, melting entire squads while you sit back and casually reload your Vector.

**Build Overview**

- **Specialization:** Field Medic (Tactical Pharma)
- **Archetype:** Engineering-stacked Oxidizing Swarm / Chemical Trap hybrid that turns team fights into EPA violations.
- **Difficulty Ceiling:** Comfortable through Challenging in PvE. Absolutely terrifying in PvP if you remember to stand behind your Vanguard instead of in front of them.
- **Core Loop:** Deploy Oxidizing Swarm → stack Tactical Layering via Chem Traps → Glass Cannon amplifies everything → Brainstorm 2 extends hive duration to ~50 seconds → enemies dissolve into neon green puddles while you reload your stat stick.

## Why This Is a Walking EPA Violation

Every Field Medic guide tells you to pick a lane — healer OR damage. ProjectPandaTv looked at that advice and said "no." This build stacks **1,700 Engineering** on a medic, runs **Glass Cannon** on a class that's supposed to keep people alive, and then casually drops an Oxidizing Swarm that lasts for nearly a minute because **Brainstorm 2** extends skill duration by +40% when allies are nearby.

The result is a build that:

- **Outlasts everything.** A 45–50 second Oxidizing Swarm in group play is an eternity. Most fights don't even last that long. Your hive is still cooking after the engagement is over.
- **Hits from every angle.** Jackpot 4pc feeds Skill Crit Chance and Skill Crit Damage. Chem Traps proc Tactical Layering for +45% Engineering. Glass Cannon multiplies the whole mess by another 28%.
- **Works everywhere.** PvE? Melts rooms. PvP? Melts agents. Dark Zone? Melts friendships. ProjectPandaTv tested this across every game mode and the kill feed doesn't discriminate.
- **Doesn't need exotic weapons.** The Lightweight M4 and First Wave Vector are standard T2 guns doing standard gun things — the damage comes from your skills, not your trigger finger.

Nobody expects the Field Medic to be the one doing the deleting. That's the point.

## Weapons

**Primary — Lightweight M4 (T2 AR)**

**Talents: In Sync + Fully Charged.** In Sync keeps your skill damage and weapon damage feeding each other — weapon hits grant +9% Skill Damage, skill hits grant +9% Weapon Damage, and when both are active they double. Fully Charged grants +7.50% Engineering per empty-magazine reload, up to 3 stacks (+22.50% total). ProjectPandaTv wants to re-roll In Sync for more Crit Damage, but material drop rates are, in his words, "miserable." Rolled for Weapon Multi-Shot + Weapon Crit Damage.

**Secondary — First Wave Vector (T2 SMG)**

**Talents: Precise Trauma 2 + Tactical Reload.** This is the gun ProjectPandaTv uses for literally everything. Precise Trauma 2 amplifies skill-based damage output, and Tactical Reload keeps the swap cycle smooth. Rolled for Skill Piercing + Skill Multi-Shot. The Vector's 1200 RPM means you dump magazines fast, which is exactly what you want when your guns exist to proc talents, not to actually kill things.

**Chest & Backpack Justification**

**Glass Cannon 2 (Chest):** +28% extra damage is just too nice. Yes, it makes you squishy, but you offset it by playing behind your team and stacking damage reduction elsewhere. Kill them before they kill you. The opportunity cost is survivability — the payoff is watching agents dissolve before they finish their ADS animation.

**Brainstorm 2 (Backpack):** Grants +40% skill duration when allies are within 20m. This is the secret sauce. This is why the Oxidizing Swarm lasts for nearly 50 seconds. ProjectPandaTv specifically tested this against Minion Training and Prime Engineer, and Brainstorm 2 won because keeping the swarm active is the entire point of the build. A dead hive does zero damage. A hive that's still cooking 45 seconds later does all the damage.

## Skill Loadout

### Oxidizing Swarm (Hive) — Main Damage

This is your bread and butter. You will drop this hive 90% of the time. The Oxidizing Swarm blankets an area in corrosive micro-drones that tick damage on everything standing in the zone. With 1,700 Engineering and Glass Cannon amplifying the output, each tick hurts. With Brainstorm 2 extending the duration to ~50 seconds in group play, the ticks never stop.

- **Mod Priorities:** Intensity > Cooldown Reduction > Critical Hit Damage > Weapon Damage > Critical Hit Chance > Multi-Shot
- **Firmware Chip:** **Hereafter Resilience** (formerly known as Acidic Effect). Departs 25% current HP to grant +35% Max HP extra health & +5% Armor for 20s. Releases micro corrosive drones every 1.6s when attached to backpack.

### Chem Traps — Tactical Layering Proc Engine

The Chem Traps exist for two purposes: area denial and Tactical Layering stacks. Every Chem Trap deployment grants +15% Engineering via the OS, stackable to 3 times (+45%). You're not just throwing traps — you're reloading your Engineering stat.

- **Mod Priorities:** Intensity > Skill Duration > Damage Reduction > Trap Radius > Critical Hit Chance
- **Firmware Chip:** **Armored Strengthening**
- *Warning:* ProjectPandaTv suspects this chip is currently bugged and not applying the armor boost. He's filed tickets, but until it's fixed, consider it a placebo.

### Skill Chip #3 — Enhanced Formula

Formerly known as Feebleness. Grants Skill Crit Damage at 2pc and debuffs enemy damage output at 3pc. ProjectPandaTv is currently stuck with a Radius roll but recommends chasing **Crit Chance, Skill Cooldown Recovery, Cooldown Reduction, or Radius.**

## OS Justification

**Tactical Layering > Surgical Engineering** for this build, and it's not close.

ProjectPandaTv considers Tactical Layering the absolute #1 OS for this setup. **What it does:** Deploying a Skill grants +15% Engineering, up to 3 stacks (+45% Engineering total).

**Why it wins:** Even though the game doesn't count dropping your Hive as a "deployment" to trigger the buff (a frustrating quirk ProjectPandaTv tested extensively on stream), the build runs **Chem Traps**. Every time you toss a Chem Trap, you gain a massive +15% Engineering stack. You can keep this stacked to 3 times just by actively using your Chem Traps, which supercharges the damage of your Oxidizing Swarm while it sits on the field melting everything.

**What about Surgical Engineering?** Surgical Engineering is the runner-up (granting Skill Crit Damage on skill crits), but the raw, scaling Engineering power of +45% from Tactical Layering simply outpaces it when you're actively managing your traps. Engineering scales everything — crit damage, base ticks, multi-shot procs. Surgical Engineering only boosts one stat. The math isn't even close.

## Combat Rotation

1. **Pre-position behind your Vanguard.** You are made of glass. Act like it. Find cover with sightlines to where enemies will cluster.
2. **Deploy Oxidizing Swarm** at the engagement zone. Brainstorm 2 immediately extends duration to ~50 seconds if allies are within 20m.
3. **Drop Chem Traps** at choke points. Each deployment triggers Tactical Layering — first trap = +15% Engineering, second = +30%, third = +45%.
4. **Fire your Lightweight M4** into the acid cloud. In Sync activates — weapon hits grant Skill Damage, skill hits grant Weapon Damage. Both sides double when active simultaneously.
5. **Swap to the First Wave Vector** for close-range encounters or when you need the faster RPM to dump magazines. Precise Trauma 2 amplifies ongoing skill damage.
6. **Maintain Chem Trap uptime.** The traps are not optional — they're your Tactical Layering fuel. Let them expire and your Engineering crashes by 45%.
7. **Re-deploy Oxidizing Swarm** the instant it expires. With Brainstorm 2, you should rarely see downtime — the hive outlasts most encounters.
8. **In PvP, drop acid on capture points and extraction zones.** Don't peek. Don't hero. Let the swarm do the fighting while you do the surviving.
