## Why This Is The Geneva Suggestion

Every Field Medic build on YouTube is a healer. Reinforcer Hive, healing chem launcher, "I keep my team alive." Noble. Boring. Wrong.

Tactical Pharma takes the Field Medic's medical expertise and repurposes it for chemical warfare. Jackpot is the *only* gear set that rolls all three skill DPS stats on its set bonuses — Skill Crit Chance, Skill Crit Damage, and Skill Multi-Shot. Fire Cycle 2pc piles on extra Skill Crit Damage just for fun.

The **Brains Over Brawn OS** converts your Engineering stat into bonus skill damage (24% at base Specialized rarity, up to 48% at max upgraded High-End). **Minion Training** backpack refreshes +36% Skill Critical Hit Damage for 12 seconds on every Skill Critical Hit Kill — and since the acid pools are critting on half their ticks across 5+ enemies, the buff is literally permanent during elite waves.

**Nothing to Lose** chest has a 50% chance when taking damage to reduce cooldowns of skills deployed within 5 seconds by 2% (stacking up to 3 times). Your Chemical Trap is up nearly 100% of the fight.

**Net Result:** A build where the skill crits ~51% of the time, multi-shots ~12% of the time, crits hit for ~+250% damage — and the skill is ALWAYS off cooldown. Your pack clear is 3-4x faster than standard weapon DPS builds.

## Skill Loadout

**Chemical Trap — Siphon (chipset)**

The Siphon chipset gives a **50% chance to restore 4 Signature Ability Charge on Corrosive stack application**. This means your Oxidizing Swarm signature comes back faster the more enemies you dissolve, which means more acid coverage, which means more Siphon procs. It's a flywheel of chemical warfare. The acid funds more acid.

**Chemical Trap — Enhanced Formula (chipset)**

Enhanced Formula boosts **Skill Crit Damage** and **restores 8% Missing HP on kill**. The Skill CHD stacks with Jackpot 3pc, Fire Cycle 2pc, and Minion Training for obscene crit damage numbers. The 8% Missing HP on kill is your only self-sustain — and it's enough, because things die fast enough that you're constantly topping off between acid pool deployments.

**Chemical Trap — Hereafter Resilience (chipset)**

Releases **micro corrosive drones from your backpack every 1.6 seconds**. This is passive, ambient acid damage that supplements your Chemical Trap pools. Think of it as your personal aura of environmental destruction — enemies near you are taking corrosive damage even between trap deployments. It's the appetizer before the main course of chemical annihilation.

**Chip Priority:** Intensity. Skip Radius and Duration. You want the acid to hurt, not to linger politely.

**Signature — Oxidizing Swarm**

Blankets the field in additional corrosive acid. Stack it on top of your Chemical Trap zones for maximum area denial. This is a damage build, not a healing one — your signature is another weapon, not a medkit.

**Brains Over Brawn > everything else** for this build. No other OS converts your primary stat directly into skill damage. With 12× Engineering mods, your Engineering stat is the single largest number on your character sheet, and Brains Over Brawn turns up to 48% of it into flat skill damage.

Other OS options either reward weapon hits (irrelevant — your guns are decorative), crit chance (already at 51% from gear), or close-range combat (you're standing behind cover watching acid pools do your job). Brains Over Brawn is the only OS where the bonus scales with the stat you're already stacking to the ceiling. It's not a choice. It's a mathematical inevitability.

## Combat Rotation

1. **Pre-engage:** Activate Brains Over Brawn — it converts 24% to 48% of your Engineering stat into bonus skill damage depending on upgrade level.
2. **Drop 2-3 Chemical Trap zones at the entry choke.** Place them where enemies funnel naturally. Watch them walk into it like moths to a very corrosive flame.
3. **The first Skill Critical Hit Kill procs Minion Training** → +36% Skill Critical Hit Damage for 12s. This buff is now permanent for the rest of the fight.
4. **Take incoming fire.** Nothing to Lose procs, shaving cooldown off your Chemical Trap. You're not dodging bullets — you're recycling them into faster acid deployment.
5. **Re-deploy acid pools the second the cooldown is up.** Nothing to Lose keeps it nearly always available. Overlap new pools with expiring ones for unbroken coverage.
6. **Pop Oxidizing Swarm** to blanket the field in even more corrosive acid. Stack it on top of your Chemical Trap zones for maximum environmental terrorism.
7. **Mop up survivors with your weapon** — if there are any. There usually aren't.
