- **Broadband required.** It is an online live service. No offline mode.

## Steam Deck Performance: Can You Actually Play It Handheld?

Yes, and it runs well. This is one of the most-searched questions around the Steam launch, so it deserves its own section.

### The Current Reality (Ubisoft Connect on Steam Deck)

You do not have to wait for the Steam launch to play Division Resurgence on Steam Deck. The community has been running the Ubisoft Connect PC version on Deck since April 2026 using the NonSteamLaunchers script and Proton-GE compatibility layer. Reports from creators and the [r/SteamDeck subreddit](https://www.reddit.com/r/SteamDeck/comments/1sy142u/the_division_resurgence_has_a_pc_port_now_and_it/) line up cleanly:

- 60 FPS on max settings is achievable on Steam Deck.
- Battery life is roughly 3 hours on the LCD Deck.
- Stutters happen but are not constant.
- Touchscreen input is a real advantage on Deck because the original UI was designed for touch.

The current install path is: Desktop Mode, install NonSteamLaunchers, install Ubisoft Connect through it, enable Proton-GE on the launcher, then sign into your Ubisoft account and launch Resurgence from inside the Ubisoft library. It is fiddly but it works.

### What the Native Steam Version Should Change

When the Steam launch lands, the install becomes a one-click Steam library entry. No more NonSteamLaunchers script, no more Proton-GE wrestling, no more "is this Ubisoft Connect game showing up in my library or not." Steam Input also opens up much richer controller mapping than the current Ubisoft Connect build offers on Deck.

Steam Deck Verified status has not been confirmed by Valve yet, but the underlying engine clearly runs well on the hardware. If the Steam build lands with a sensible default control scheme, Verified or Playable status is the most likely outcome.

## Controller Support on Steam: PS5 DualSense, Xbox, Backbone, and Steam Input

The game supports **PlayStation 5 DualSense controllers** with native button mapping, **Xbox Wireless Controllers** (Series X/S, One generation), and **Backbone One** mobile-style grips. Generic Bluetooth pads work but may need manual remapping. All of this is already live on the Ubisoft Connect PC build today, per the [official Ubisoft Play Resurgence on PC info page](https://www.ubisoft.com/en-gb/game/the-division/resurgence/news-updates/1rUYWPgMV9hXYZ6FcLOkpX/play-resurgence-on-pc-all-you-need-to-know).

### What Steam Input Adds at Launch

Once the Steam version is live, Steam Input adds an entire layer of controller customization on top of the in-game settings:

- Full key remapping for any controller, including older Xbox 360 pads, Switch Pro controllers, generic Bluetooth pads, and the Steam Controller.
- Per-game profiles you can share with the community through the Steam Workshop equivalent.
- Gyro aim support on Steam Deck, DualSense, and Switch Pro controllers.
- Touchpad mappings on DualSense and Steam Deck for menu navigation, hotkey wheels, or skill quick-select.
- Activator chords (double-tap, hold, long-press) for cramming more bindings onto the same physical buttons.

This is the single biggest practical reason a hardcore PC player should wait for Steam. The Ubisoft Connect build accepts controllers, but Steam Input takes the binding flexibility to a level no separate launcher matches.

### Mouse and Keyboard Are Also Fully Supported

Yes, the Ubisoft Connect PC version supports mouse and keyboard, and the Steam version will continue that support out of the box. You can rebind keys, adjust sensitivity, and play the game the way a traditional looter-shooter PC player expects to play it. The original UI was built for touch, so a few interface elements feel a little chunky on a desktop monitor, but core combat, inventory management, and map navigation all work cleanly with M+KB.

## How to Play Division Resurgence on PC Today (Ubisoft Connect Path Before Steam)

If you do not want to wait for the Steam launch, here is the exact install path that works right now on Windows 10 and Windows 11.

1. Download and install the **Ubisoft Connect launcher** from [ubisoft.com/ubisoft-connect](https://www.ubisoft.com/en-us/ubisoft-connect).
2. Sign into your Ubisoft Connect account, or create one if you do not have one yet.
3. In the Ubisoft Connect store, search for **The Division Resurgence**.
4. Click install. The launcher first downloads a smaller launcher, then prompts you to download the full game files.
5. When the install finishes, click play and sign in with the same Ubisoft Connect account.

If you already play on mobile, sign in with the **same** Ubisoft Connect account you use on your phone. Your progression, your [exotics](/guides/division-resurgence-exotic-weapons-list-2026/), your Classified Ops Pass tracker, all of it will be there waiting.

Total time from "I want to start" to "I am in a mission" is about 30 to 45 minutes depending on your internet speed. The 17 GB download is the longest part.

When the Steam version goes live, this same Ubisoft Connect account is what you will sign in with on Steam. Your progress carries forward. You do not start over.

## For New PC Agents Arriving Through Steam: A 5-Minute Quickstart

If Steam is going to be your entry point and this is your first time in Division Resurgence, here is the absolute minimum you need to know to not get lost on day one.

**1. Pick a server region carefully.** North America, Europe, and Asia are isolated servers. You cannot transfer between them. Friends play together only when they are on the same regional server. Pick once, pick correctly.

**2. Pick your specialization based on playstyle.** [Vanguard](/guides/which-specialization-to-pick/) is the weapon-and-support class. Demolitionist is the weapon-and-skill class. Field Medic is the healer-tank hybrid. Bulwark is the heavy frame. You can experiment with all of them in the early game before committing to a main.

**3. The main loop is daily quests, story missions, Dark Zone runs, and Conflicts PvP.** Dailies give the best XP-per-minute. Story missions unlock new zones. Dark Zone is the loot-extraction PvPvE area. Conflicts is structured PvP (Skirmish team deathmatch and Domination point capture).

**4. Upgrade your gear instead of constantly swapping.** Power Score scales with upgrade investment, not just rarity. Pick one set early and invest in it before chasing every new drop.

**5. Read the patch notes.** Resurgence is on a fast seasonal cadence. The current patch is **1.1.2.2**. The build meta shifts faster than most live-service shooters.

## Common Steam Launch Questions

### Will the Steam version require Ubisoft Connect to run?

This has not been formally confirmed for the Steam release, but every other Steam-published Ubisoft title in the last several years has launched a Ubisoft Connect background process when you start the game. The most likely outcome here is that the Steam version of Resurgence does the same, with a single Ubisoft Connect login on first launch and silent account linking after that. We will update this article when Ubisoft confirms.

### Can I transfer my Ubisoft Connect progress to Steam?

Almost certainly yes, because progression is tied to your Ubisoft Connect account rather than to the storefront you launched from. The Steam version is most likely a Ubisoft Connect game that you happen to start through Steam. We are waiting on an official statement before stating this as confirmed fact.

### Is Division Resurgence pay-to-win on Steam?

No. The monetization is cosmetic and battle-pass-driven. There is no paywall blocking power. You can fully build a top-tier character without spending a dollar. Skins, outfits, and the seasonal Classified Ops Pass premium track are optional purchases. The Steam version inherits the same model.

### Will Division Resurgence get a console release on PS5 or Switch 2?

No console release has been announced as of June 15, 2026. The confirmed platforms are iOS, Android, PC via Ubisoft Connect, and PC via Steam (later in 2026). Console requests are loud in the community, but Ubisoft has not commented on a console version.

### How big is the Steam install?

The Ubisoft Connect version is about 17 GB initial download. The Steam page lists 30 GB available storage required, which gives headroom for patches and seasonal content. The Steam download is expected to be in the same range as the Ubisoft Connect build.

### Is there an offline mode on Steam?

No. Resurgence is an always-online live service game. Broadband internet is required regardless of which storefront you launched it from.

### Will old Ubisoft Connect Early Access progress carry into the full Steam launch?

Yes. Per Ubisoft's PC info page, the current PC build is technical Early Access and all progression earned during it carries forward to the full launch. You are not playing on a throwaway character.

### How does the Steam version compare to mobile in terms of features?

Functionally identical. Crossplay means PC and mobile players share matchmaking pools, the same Dark Zones, the same Conflicts queues, the same world events. The Steam version adds mouse and keyboard input, full controller flexibility through Steam Input, larger screens, and the ability to play without thermal-throttling your phone into a brick.

### Does Division Resurgence work on Steam Deck?

Yes, today, through the Ubisoft Connect on Steam Deck workaround (NonSteamLaunchers script plus Proton-GE compatibility). Performance is solid: 60 FPS at max settings is achievable, battery life is roughly 3 hours on the LCD model, touchscreen input is a real advantage for the mobile-derived UI. The native Steam launch should simplify the install to a single library entry.

### Will my mobile builds work on the Steam version?

Yes. Same game, same [build planner](/builder/), same gear, same patch (currently 1.1.2.2). Our [Build Planner](/builder/) generates URLs that work for both mobile and PC players. Top community builds all translate one-to-one to the PC client.

### Will Steam Achievements be available at launch?

Not officially confirmed yet. Steam Achievements are standard on Ubisoft titles launched on Steam, so the most likely outcome is yes. We are waiting on the formal Steam Achievements list.

### Can I refund a free-to-play game on Steam?

Free-to-play titles cannot really be "refunded" because there is no purchase. Any cosmetics or premium battle-pass purchases follow Steam's standard refund policy (under 2 hours played and within 14 days). Refunds on consumable currency are at Steam's discretion.

### Will the Steam version support ultrawide monitors?

Not officially confirmed for ultrawide aspect ratios (21:9, 32:9) at the Steam launch. Standard 16:9 and 16:10 will absolutely be supported. We will update this section if Ubisoft posts ultrawide details closer to launch.

### How do I follow Steam launch updates?

Wishlist on Steam to get a launch-day notification from Steam directly. Follow the [@TheDivMobile X account](https://x.com/TheDivMobile) for first-party announcements. Bookmark this guide and we will update it the same day any Steam-specific news lands.
