Three exotic weapons are currently live in Division Resurgence as of Patch 1.1.2.2: **Lady Death** (SMG), **Warlord** (Assault Rifle), and **Strawberry Milkshake** (LMG). One more is officially confirmed for Phase 3 in June 2026: **Scorpio** (SMG). One more is leaked from a Reddit screenshot but not officially confirmed: **Diamondback** (class unconfirmed). This page is the single source of truth for every exotic in Resurgence, what it does, and how it stacks up against the rest.

## What Counts as an Exotic in Division Resurgence

Exotic weapons are the rarest weapon rarity in Resurgence. Each one carries a fixed unique talent that cannot be recalibrated, which is what separates them from High-End weapons. The talent is the entire reason the exotic exists. The stats can be optimized at the Optimization Station, but the talent text is locked the moment the weapon drops. If you get a bad stat roll, you are farming another copy.

Most exotics in Resurgence are designed to break a specific rule of the game. Some let you stack debuffs that transfer between enemies. Others let you stack damage reduction while firing. Others build movement-based stack systems that pay out raw weapon damage. They are not always the highest raw DPS weapon in the slot, but they are almost always the most build-defining.

### Lady Death · Exotic SMG

| Stat | Value |
|------|-------|
| Class | SMG |
| Damage | 299 Blast Damage |
| RPM | 900 |
| Magazine | 32 |
| Accuracy | 50 |
| Reload Speed | 2.98s |

**Talent · Breathe Free**
"While moving, grants 4 stacks per second, or 8 stacks if sprinting, up to a maximum of 40 stacks. A stack is consumed with each round fired, granting +15% Weapon Damage. Deploying a Skill consumes 10 stacks and grants +15% Skill Damage. Kills grant +10% Movement Speed for 10 seconds."

Lady Death is the movement-rewarding exotic of the current pool. Sprint to charge up to 40 stacks, then dump the 32-round magazine for +15% Weapon Damage per round fired. Every shot fired consumes one stack, so a full magazine consumes 32 of the 40 available. The math says you want to be sprinting between engagements, anchoring just long enough to empty the mag, then moving again to recharge.

The Skill-Damage clause is the part that makes this weapon flex into hybrid builds. Deploying a Skill consumes 10 stacks for +15% Skill Damage, which lets a Tech Operator or a Field Medic squeeze offensive value out of the gun even on builds that aren't pure weapon damage.

**Talent · Play Rough**
"While firing, reduces Incoming Damage by 25%. When firing ceases, applies 60% of the reduced damage and grants +10% Skill Damage for 10 seconds."

Warlord is the brawler exotic. The talent design is straight out of a gamer-dad fever dream: pull the trigger and you eat 25% less damage. Stop firing, you take 60% of what you would have eaten as delayed damage, plus you get +10% Skill Damage for 10 seconds. The risk-reward is clean. Aggressive players sustain through fights by never breaking trigger discipline, then bank the Skill Damage buff for a turret or drone follow-up.

Warlord has a clean 29m optimal range that makes it usable across the PvE arena. It was the Phase 1 free exotic on the Classified Ops Pass, which is the easiest path to unlock if you were active during the launch window.

| Stat | Value |

| Damage | 300 Shredding Damage |
| RPM | 550 |
| Magazine | 100 |
| Accuracy | 74 |

**Talent · Anarchy**
"Hits inflict a debuff, dealing 2.40% Weapon Damage over 4.50 seconds, up to 50 stacks. Enemies dying with this debuff transfer all stacks to a nearby enemy within 12 meters. "

The Strawberry Milkshake is one of the cleanest debuff designs in the franchise. Every hit stacks a damage-over-time debuff on the target. Fifty stacks is the cap, and at that point the target is hemorrhaging health every tick. The transfer clause is what makes it broken. When the target dies, every stack transfers to a nearby enemy within 12 meters. In a packed Hostile Nest or a Weekly Bounty boss room with adds, you are essentially turning every elite or boss into a stack reservoir that propagates through the entire pack.

This is the **Phase 2 free exotic** of the Classified Ops Pass. If you ran the Phase 2 pass tiers, the Milkshake unlocks on the free track. It is also the centerpiece of the [Milkshake Machine LMG build](/builds/milkshake-machine-strawberry-milkshake-lmg-build/), a Vanguard-focused build that pairs Phalanx Attack 4-piece with Fury Strike 2-piece to push the debuff math past the point where Hostile Nest bosses melt.

## Confirmed Upcoming Exotics

### Scorpio · Exotic SMG · Phase 3 (June 2026)

The Scorpio is the confirmed Phase 3 free exotic of Classified Ops Pass 1.3. Ubisoft put it on the official Phase 3 marketing alongside the Freemen apparel set and the Boombox weapon skin. The weapon class is confirmed as an **SMG**. The talent name, stat profile, and tooltip text have not been published yet.

Phase 3 launches in **June 2026** per Ubisoft's official roadmap. Community trackers have been pointing at **June 23, 2026** as the rollover date based on the roughly six-week phase cadence the season has been holding to (Phase 1 ran from launch through May 11, Phase 2 kicked off May 12). June 23 is not an official Ubisoft date, just the cleanest community estimate.

A quick word on the name. In Division 2 on PC and console, "Scorpio" is the name of an exotic shotgun built on the SIX12 chassis. The Resurgence Scorpio is a different weapon class (SMG), not the same weapon. Treat them as separate weapons that share a name.

## How to Get Exotic Weapons in Division Resurgence

Exotic weapons drop from specific activities. Some are tied to mission bosses, others come from the Dark Zone, others come from special events. Drop rates are low, so expect to grind for the ones not tied to a pass. A handful of exotics have dedicated quest lines you can track through the **Projects tab** in the menu, which is the cleanest way to figure out if a specific exotic has a guaranteed quest path or if you are running into RNG.

The free exotics of the Classified Ops Pass (Warlord in Phase 1, Strawberry Milkshake in Phase 2, Scorpio in Phase 3) do not require Dark Zone runs or boss grinds. You unlock them by progressing the pass tiers, which any agent can do on the free track. The fastest known path to pass XP is the Weekly Bounty system at 120 SPXP per bounty and the Chinatown Rescue mission on Challenging at 210 SPXP per run.

## Can You Recalibrate Exotic Weapons?

No. Exotic weapons have fixed talents. You cannot recalibrate the unique ability. You can still optimize the base stats at the Optimization Station, but the talent text is locked. If you roll an exotic with bad stat lines, you are farming another copy to chase a cleaner roll.

## What is the Best Exotic Weapon in Division Resurgence?

It shifts every patch. The strongest exotics are the ones whose unique talent synergizes with a specific build archetype. An exotic with a mediocre talent on paper can be S-tier if it enables a broken combo with certain gear sets or skills.

For Patch 1.1.2.2, the strongest published combos on resurgencebuilds.com use each exotic differently:

- **Strawberry Milkshake** anchors the [Milkshake Machine LMG build](/builds/milkshake-machine-strawberry-milkshake-lmg-build/), where Anarchy's 50-stack debuff plus the kill-transfer clause turns a Hostile Nest into a chain reaction
- **Warlord** carries the Play Rough damage reduction window for any build that wants to sustain in extended firefights while banking a Skill Damage payoff for skill rotations
- **Lady Death** is the natural fit for mobility-focused builds that already want to be moving between cover positions, since Breathe Free pays out the most when you are sprinting between engagements

The Scorpio is the next likely meta shift. Once it lands in June with its tooltip, we will reshuffle this list.
