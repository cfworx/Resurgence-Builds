## Welcome, Agent

The Division Resurgence has a lot of systems and not all of them are obvious. This guide covers everything you need to know to avoid wasting time and resources during your first 30 levels and beyond.

## The Daily XP Boost — Don't Ignore This

You'll notice an orange arrow icon near your level indicator. This means your daily XP boost is active. When the icon turns gray, you've hit your XP threshold for the day and your gain rate drops significantly.

**The boost does not carry over between days.** Play every day to maximize your leveling speed, even if it's just 30-60 minutes of focused activity.

## The 10-Activity Daily Cap

Only **10 open-world activities per daily reset** grant full rewards. After the 10th activity, reward drops stop. This means you should prioritize high-value activities first:

1. Main story missions (always reward regardless of cap)
2. Faction nests (3 rotating maps, good XP and loot)
3. Dynamic world events and patrols
4. Side missions (unlock via safe houses)

## Picking Your First Specialization

Four specializations are available from the start:

| Specialization | Role | Best For Beginners? |
|---------------|------|-------------------|
| Demolitionist | Burst DPS / Crowd Control | **Yes** — Artillery Turret + Seeker Mine makes solo play easy |
| Bulwark | Tank / Frontline | Good for aggressive players who like face-tanking |
| Vanguard | Recon / Tactical | Better for PvP-focused players |
| Field Medic | Healer / Support | Best in groups, harder solo |

**Tech Operator** (Drone Specialist) unlocks via progression and is excellent for sustained DPS once available.

You can switch specializations at the Base of Operations Coordinator without losing progress. Each spec has a skill tree requiring 49 points to max out (30 from Specialization XP, 19 from Shade Tech Caches found in world events).

## Understanding Gear Tiers

Gear drops in color-coded tiers:

| Tier | Color | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| Standard | Green | Vendor trash past level 10 |
| Specialized | Blue | Usable through mid-game |
| Superior | Purple | Endgame-viable with good rolls |
| High-End | Gold | Primary endgame gear |
| Prototype | Red/Orange | Highest tier, has passive "Augment" perks |

**Every slot** has weapons, gear pieces, and mods. Don't ignore mods — 2-piece and 3-piece mod set bonuses can transform your build.

## The Loot System

Unlike Division 1 and 2, items in Resurgence can be **upgraded with resources up to your current player level**. This means a well-rolled Purple at level 15 can carry you to level 25 if you keep upgrading it. Don't scrap great rolls just because they're lower level.

### Stat Priorities by Role

- **DPS builds:** Firepower Core → Weapon Damage, Crit Chance, Crit Damage
- **Tank builds:** Toughness Core → Armor, Max Health, Damage Reduction
- **Healer builds:** Engineering Core → Skill Intensity, Skill Duration, Skill CDR
- **Solo builds:** Mix of Firepower + Toughness; self-sustain talents

## Crafting — Set Your Expectations

Crafting uses a **24-hour real-time timer** per craft. This is not a bug — the progress bar moves in real time. Crafting is **not** the primary way to get gear. Deconstruction for materials + targeted farming is far more efficient.

Use crafting for:
- Filling a specific gear slot you can't get to drop
- Rolling for specific talents on a piece

## The Dark Zone — Before You Go In

The Dark Zone is a PvPvE area. Rules to survive:

1. **Contaminated loot must be extracted** via helicopter at extraction points. Other players can attack you during extraction
2. **Going rogue** means attacking other players. You gain rogue status and become a target
3. **If the game crashes during extraction, your contaminated loot is lost.** Extract early and often
4. The **Dark Zone Vendor** has daily rotating stock with exclusive items including exotic weapons like Lady Death
5. A **heroic boss** respawns every 10 minutes in the center of the DZ — good for farming but contested

### Tips for DZ Survival

- Extract early, don't hoard contaminated items
- Watch your extraction timer — there's a known desync of 1-2 seconds vs. server time
- Stick with a group if possible
- Learn the extraction points and plan your route before entering
- The DZ is where targeted loot farming becomes most efficient at endgame

## Network Issues — Quick Fixes

If you're hitting Error 602 or connection drops:

1. Switch between Wi-Fi and mobile data
2. Change DNS to `1.1.1.1` or `8.8.8.8`
3. Clear the game's cache
4. If switching from 5G to Wi-Fi mid-session causes disconnects, lock to one connection type
5. VPN can help (Germany/Netherlands servers recommended)

## Daily Routine for Efficient Play

Once you've got the basics down, here's an optimal daily routine:

1. **Check daily rewards** at login
2. **Run main story missions** if available (best XP, not capped)
3. **Complete 10 high-value open-world activities** (faction nests > patrols > events)
4. **Run Dark Zone** for targeted loot farming (if geared enough)
5. **Do Lone Wolf Challenges** at the highest tier you can clear
6. **Queue for Conflict PvP** for additional rewards
7. **Check the Dark Zone Vendor** for daily stock rotation
8. **Craft anything** with a 24h timer before logging off

Play every day while the XP boost is active. Consistency beats marathon sessions.
